## 7. Peer-Reviewed Academic Studies

### AEI attendance impact study (April 2026)
40. **Malkus N, Hollon S. *An Extra Point for Attendance: The Impact of High School Varsity Athletics on Absenteeism*. American Enterprise Institute, April 8, 2026.** [AEI report page](https://www.aei.org/research-products/report/an-extra-point-for-attendance-the-impact-of-high-school-varsity-athletics-on-absenteeism/). [Full PDF](https://www.aei.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/An-Extra-Point-for-Attendance-1.pdf). n=262,600 Indiana HS students; varsity athletes 10.0% chronically absent vs. non-athletes 22.9% (−7.7 pp adjusted effect). Effect ~2× larger for FRPM-eligible students. **Primary source for the LCFF-attendance argument in Pillar 5 and Exec Summary finding #7.**
41. [EdWeek — *The Lesson Schools Can Learn From Student-Athletes' Attendance Data*](https://www.edweek.org/leadership/the-lesson-schools-can-learn-from-student-athletes-attendance-data/2026/04) — education-press coverage of the Malkus & Hollon study.
42. [Washington Post — *Student-athletes more likely to attend school than peers, new study finds*](https://www.washingtonpost.com/education/2026/04/08/student-athletes-have-better-attendance-study/) (April 8, 2026, paywalled) — general-press coverage of the Malkus & Hollon study.

### Injury / Athletic Trainer impact study
43. **Pierpoint LA, Williams CM, Fields SK, Comstock RD. *Injuries in girls' soccer and basketball: a comparison of high schools with and without athletic trainers*. Injury Epidemiology 5:29, 2018.** [Full text](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40621-018-0159-6). Source of the **6× / 3× recurrent injury rate ratios** for no-AT vs. AT schools in girls' soccer and girls' basketball — the core student-safety finding in Pillar 4.

### CA AT staffing prevalence study
44. **Pickett et al. *Factors Influencing Athletic Training Services in California Secondary Schools: A 5-Year Update*. *Journal of Athletic Training*, 2024.** [PMC article](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11611368/). Findings: 51.6% of CA schools no AT; 45.0% of CA public schools have certified AT; 10.5% of CA public schools use unqualified personnel.
45. [Korey Stringer Institute — ATLAS Project (NFHS survey infrastructure)](https://koreystringer.institute.uconn.edu/atlas/).

## Methodology notes on sources

1. **Primary-source verification**: Every dollar figure in the report traces to either (a) a Board-approved salary schedule, (b) a posted EdJoin job listing, (c) a governing CBA, (d) CDE audited current expense data, or (e) the CHSSO 2024–26 fee document. Secondary-source-only figures are flagged in the brief.
2. **Google Drive-hosted primary documents** (Sweetwater C-2, Helix HTA schedules, Steele Canyon CBA) have been directly reviewed; links are to the owning association/district's official posting where available.
3. **Image-based PDFs that couldn't be text-extracted** (e.g., the OUSD/OTA 25MB contract): dollar figures were confirmed via EdJoin job postings that reproduce the schedule amounts.
4. **Remaining gaps (honestly labeled)**: SMUSD per-class stipend dollar amounts are not in the CBA body; SDUSD coaching stipend appendix is not in the main CBA; peer SD County districts' ASB-vs.-general-fund officials split requires AD-to-AD survey.
