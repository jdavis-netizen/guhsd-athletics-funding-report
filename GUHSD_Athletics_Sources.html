Category 7

Peer-Reviewed Academic Studies

AEI attendance impact study (April 2026)

  1. Malkus N, Hollon S. An Extra Point for Attendance: The Impact of High School Varsity Athletics on Absenteeism. American Enterprise Institute, April 8, 2026. AEI report page · Full PDF. n=262,600 Indiana HS students; varsity athletes 10.0% chronically absent vs. non-athletes 22.9% (−7.7 pp adjusted). Effect ~2× larger for FRPM-eligible students. Primary source for the LCFF-attendance argument in Pillar 5 and Exec Summary finding #7.
  2. EdWeek coverage.
  3. Washington Post coverage (paywalled).

Injury / Athletic Trainer impact study

  1. Pierpoint LA, Williams CM, Fields SK, Comstock RD. Injuries in girls' soccer and basketball: a comparison of high schools with and without athletic trainers. Injury Epidemiology 5:29, 2018. Full text. Source of the 6× / 3× recurrent injury rate ratios for no-AT vs. AT schools — core student-safety finding in Pillar 4.

CA AT staffing prevalence study

  1. Pickett et al. Factors Influencing Athletic Training Services in California Secondary Schools: A 5-Year Update. Journal of Athletic Training, 2024. PMC article. 51.6% of CA schools no AT; 45.0% of CA publics have certified AT; 10.5% of CA publics use unqualified personnel.
  2. Korey Stringer Institute — ATLAS Project (NFHS survey infrastructure).
  3. Training & Conditioning coverage of Pickett study.
