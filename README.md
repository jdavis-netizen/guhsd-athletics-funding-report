# GUHSD Athletics Funding: A Case for District Support

An evidence-based review of athletics funding at Grossmont Union High School District compared to eight San Diego County peer districts and charters, prepared April 2026 for the GUHSD Governing Board by Granite Hills Athletics Administration.

## Scope

Three funding pillars analyzed:

1. **Coaching stipends and slot allocation** — head varsity rates, assistant coverage, Athletic Director compensation
2. **Athletic trainer staffing** — how the AT role is funded and certified
3. **Officials fees** — district allocation vs. actual cost, and the ASB/booster gap

Eight San Diego County districts and charters verified against primary-source documents:

- Grossmont Union HSD
- Sweetwater Union HSD
- San Dieguito UHSD
- Escondido UHSD
- San Marcos Unified
- Oceanside Unified
- Helix Charter HS
- Steele Canyon Charter HS

Plus California-wide context via the California Department of Education's *Current Expense of Education per ADA*, the 2022–23 ATLAS survey of athletic training in California secondary schools, and AB 796 (2024, title protection for athletic trainers).

## Headline finding

GUHSD has the 3rd-highest per-ADA spending in San Diego County ($21,644/ADA per FY 2023–24 CDE data) and holds general fund reserves ~4× the state-mandated minimum — yet its athletics funding trails peer districts that spend up to 28% less per student. The underfunding is allocative, not resource-constrained. The district's own Athletic Trainer Staffing and Financial Realignment Proposal puts the full corrective package at roughly $1.4M/year (0.44% of the general fund; 3–5% of reserves).

## Sourcing

Every numeric figure in these documents traces to a primary source — board-approved CBAs, district salary schedules, CDE audited expenditure reports, or verified EdJoin job postings. Gaps and inference limits are called out explicitly.
