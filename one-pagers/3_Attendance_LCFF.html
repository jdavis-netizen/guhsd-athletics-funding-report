Athletics Drives ADA
GUHSD Board Brief Granite Hills Athletics
April 2026
A new peer-reviewed study (April 2026, n=262,600) finds varsity athletes are 12.9 percentage points less likely to be chronically absent than non-athletes — and the effect is ~2× larger for low-income students. In California, where LCFF revenue is ADA-based, athletics is one of the most direct attendance interventions a district has.

The math: why the $1.4M proposal pays for itself

  • 1 percentage point of district-wide attendance gain = 148 ADA × $11,500–$13,000 LCFF rate (HS grades 9-12) = $1.7M – $1.9M per year in LCFF revenue
  • The full $1.4M athletics proposal = LESS than the LCFF revenue from a single percentage-point of attendance improvement
  • Break-even threshold = roughly 0.7–0.9 percentage points of district-wide attendance lift — well within the AEI's documented 7.7 pp varsity-athlete effect
  • And the effect is larger for the supplemental/concentration LCFF student population (FRPM-eligible at 1.45 pp), so retaining or expanding athletics participation in the lowest-income students disproportionately drives LCFF supplemental dollars

Athletics participation is rising — even as enrollment declines

  • CA HS enrollment has declined 8 consecutive years (~1.3%/yr). CIF participation: 763,867 → 806,979 → 852,575 (2024-25 all-time high; +11.6% two-year).
  • In a declining-enrollment, ADA-driven funding environment, athletics is one of the few programs whose participation base is growing while overall enrollment shrinks — exactly where retention investment has the highest LCFF return.
The Ask
Approve the full $1.4M athletics proposal package as a documented attendance/retention investment. The AEI evidence + ADA-based LCFF math make this the only board action where the cost is structurally less than the revenue it can generate.
$1.4M = 0.44% of $321M general fund · 3–5% of $30–45M reserves · break-even at <1 pp district-wide attendance gain.
