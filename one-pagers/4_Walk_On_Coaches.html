What "walk-on" means in California public schools

  • Teacher-coach: A district-employed credentialed teacher who also accepts a coaching stipend on the certificated extra-duty schedule. Compensation paid through district payroll. Fully background-cleared via the credentialing process.
  • Walk-on coach: Non-teacher contracted as an hourly or per-event coach. Compensation paid via stipend or hourly contract. Background clearance and on-campus work authorization through California's Activity Supervisor Clearance Certificate (ASCC) framework, codified in AB 346 (Cal. Ed. Code §35021.1) — fingerprint clearance + child abuse training + coaching education.
  • How the shift happens: When stipend caps prevent funding additional teacher-coaches, schools fill the gap with walk-ons. Over successive years, the program drifts toward majority walk-on.

The ADA-retention loop

  • Stipend caps → programs fill positions with walk-ons → lower on-campus presence + continuity + academic visibility → reduced student retention in the program → reduced ADA.
  • This is how Helix and Steele Canyon's higher coaching investment compounds: more teacher-coaches → more program continuity → more student retention → more ADA. Both charters explicitly require CA teaching credential for top-tier coaching pay (Helix HTA §13.1.4.1; SC 2025-26 schedule).
The Ask · Two-part data + policy
(1) Data ask: Pull GUHSD's certificated-stipend vs. ASCC-cleared walk-on coach ratio from Business Services payroll — single-query. (2) Policy ask: Retire the rigid 52-position per-school stipend cap in favor of sport-specific allocations (mirror Sweetwater & SMUSD) so that programs can fund teacher-coaches at the level the sport actually requires, before falling back on walk-ons.
Estimated cost of expanding stipended slots by ~2 per school district-wide: $96,714/year (per GUHSD's own internal proposal) — under 0.03% of the $321M general fund.
