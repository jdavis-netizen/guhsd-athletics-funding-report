The $35/Hour Trainer
GUHSD Board Brief Granite Hills Athletics
April 2026
GUHSD pays Athletic Trainers $35/hour — set in 2021–22 and unchanged since. The California Athletic Trainers Association recommends a minimum of $67–$73/hour for our region. Six of seven SD County peers employ AT as a classified healthcare role with benefits and BOC certification required.

Three independent arguments for AT funding restructure

  1. Student safety (Pierpoint 2018, peer-reviewed). Schools without certified ATs see 6× higher recurrent injury rates in girls' soccer and 3× in girls' basketball. Four GUHSD sites (El Cajon Valley, Mount Miguel, El Capitan, Santana) currently rely on walk-on trainers.
  2. Title IX exposure (U.S. Dept. of Education OCR, Morgan Hill USD, October 2023). The OCR resolution explicitly requires "equitable access to the school's athletic trainer" regardless of booster-funding source. GUHSD's split model (5 internal CTE + 4 walk-on sites + ASB-funded supplements) is structurally analogous to the model OCR ruled non-compliant.
  3. Market-rate / professional standards (CATA 2022). GUHSD pays roughly half of CATA's recommended floor for any region of California — and well below half the appropriate rate for cost-of-living-adjusted Southern California.

The structural coverage gap (verified directly with current GUHSD AT)

ElementContractedActual / Impact
Months of work9 months10.5–11 months actually worked — 1.5–2 uncompensated months/year
Hours per season cap214 hours × 3 seasons = 642 hoursAverage ~17 hrs/week; football and winter sports exceed that
Coverage shortfall—Spring sports get less AT coverage because high-demand fall/winter sports consume more than the per-week budget — a structural funding gap, not an effort gap
"ATs are currently contracted/paid for 9 months of work, but we end up working 10.5/11 months. We are currently being paid $35/hour to work 214 hours per season (there are 3 seasons). This averages out to about 17 hours per week. However, certain sports/seasons require more than 17 hours per week (such as football, winter sports, etc.) which leaves less hours for other sports/seasons." — Katherine Leek, Granite Hills HS Athletic Trainer (April 2026)
The Ask · GUHSD Athletics' own costed proposal
Approve the Athletic Trainer Staffing & Financial Realignment proposal: Track 1 retention of 5 internal CTE-AT sites + Track 2 ATvantage Athletic Training vendor at the 4 walk-on sites = 100% certified AT coverage at every campus.
Total ongoing cost: $765,000/year (Option A, full-time vendor) = 0.24% of $321M general fund · 1.7–2.6% of $30–45M reserves · plus $12,670 one-time retroactive COLA correction.
