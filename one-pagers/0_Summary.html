GUHSD Athletics Funding — At a Glance
GUHSD Board Brief Granite Hills Athletics
April 2026
GUHSD is not resource-constrained. It is the 3rd-highest per-student-spending HSD in San Diego County with general-fund reserves ~4× the state mandate. What is constrained is the share of those resources reaching athletics — and the gap is being closed by family booster fundraising and student ASB trusts, not the district. Total ongoing fix: ~$1.4M / yr (≈ 0.44% of general fund).

The six findings

#TopicKey MetricOne-line finding
1 Athletic Trainers $35 / hr GUHSD pays its AT a $22,500 stipend (~$35/hr); CATA recommends $67–$73/hr for SoCal Region 5–6. Six of seven verified SD County peers staff AT as full-time classified. Pierpoint 2018 (6× / 3× injury risk) and Morgan Hill OCR 2023 (Title IX) make this a safety + legal exposure, not just a market issue.
2 Coaching Stipends $1,190–$1,640 below Helix Charter (in the Grossmont League) pays head varsity football $1,190–$1,640 more than GUHSD at every experience tier (2025-26 schedule). Sweetwater pays 40% more at entry. AD comparison: GUHSD second-lowest in the SD County peer set at $6,227 top tier vs. Helix $13,675 / Steele Canyon $13,434.
3 Attendance & LCFF +$1.7M / pp ADA AEI April 2026 study (n=262,600): athletes 12.9 pp less likely to be chronically absent — effect 2× larger for low-income students. 1 pp attendance ≈ $1.7M+ in CA LCFF revenue. Athletics is a revenue-generating intervention, not a cost center.
4 Walk-On Coaches 52-slot cap The 52-stipend cap (across 24+ sports) forces programs to fill rosters with walk-ons — LAUSD reports 58% walk-on prevalence. Erodes teacher-coach retention, on-campus visibility, supervision continuity. At Granite Hills, 52 stipends are split across 58 coaches just to cover staffing.
5 Officials & Ref Fees 38% district share GUHSD allocates $22,000/school. Granite Hills FY 2025-26 verified competition overhead: $57,393. District covers 38%; ASB / boosters cover the remaining $35,393. CHSSO 2025-26 fee schedule verified for every major sport.
6 Cost-Shifting via ASB $96,009 Granite Hills is paying $96,009 out of trust accounts to compensate 53 coaches the district won't put on stipend — formally authorized via the GUHSD "Request To Pay Coach – Trust" Google Form, routed through district HR via PAF, but funded entirely by ASB. + $27,710 non-league officials/clearance/etc. = $123,719+ verified ASB cost-shift at one site.
The reframe. This brief does not argue the district lacks money. It argues the district has above-peer per-ADA funding, above-mandate reserves, and below-peer athletics allocation — and that the resulting gap is pushed onto student ASBs and family-led booster fundraising in a way that is not happening at peer districts with less to work with. Three independent sources (Pierpoint 2018, Morgan Hill OCR 2023, AEI April 2026) make this a student-safety, Title IX, and ADA-revenue case — not just a coach-pay case.
The Ask · Five steps · ~$1.4M / yr ongoing
(1) Reclassify Athletic Trainer to full-time classified at CATA-recommended rate. (2) Lift the 52-stipend cap and bring booster-paid coaches onto district payroll (already routed through HR via PAF — funded from wrong source). (3) Match the AD / ASB Advisor / Band Director compensation tier to verified peers (Helix, Steele Canyon, Sweetwater). (4) Raise the per-school officials allocation from $22K to ~$40K (closes ~half the ASB officials gap). (5) Stop separately invoicing ASB for coach clearance, LiveScan, league patches, and non-league officials.
Total cost: ~$1.4M / yr ≈ 0.44% of the GUHSD general fund · 3–5% of district reserves · closes roughly 80% of the verified district-wide booster burden ($1.0M–$1.4M+).
