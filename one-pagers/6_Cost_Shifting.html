What this changes about the funding-gap argument

  • Previously-documented "officials gap" at Granite Hills was $35,393 (Arbiter + tournament fees only). Adding $73,447 in verified district-to-ASB billings → $108,840+ booster burden through 4/29 (~3× the previously-stated figure). Partial-year — spring-sport coach pay posts in May/June; year-end projection $125K+. Scaled district-wide: $300K–$700K+ in cost-shifting, on top of the $162K–$297K officials-only burden.
  • The "52 stipends per school" cap isn't the real coaching-pay constraint — coaches above the cap are paid via the district BILLING the booster trust. Verified mechanism: a single PO #8996 invoice billed Football's ASB $11,759.73 for 7 named coaches; similar invoices in Wrestling, Cheer, Baseball, etc. Coach Clearance ($70/coach), Non-League Officials, League Patches, LiveScan, NFHS — all separately invoiced to ASB.
  • Granite Hills' $20K AD Athletic Needs Fund (#5032-06) is exhausted — $26,391 committed by 4/29/2026 against $20K budget (with 2 months left in FY). Covered routine equipment, tournament fees, and 3 GUHSD "Athletic Needs" invoices — items the district budget should cover.
The Ask · Stop the cost-shifting
(1) Move Coach Pay billing INTO the $1.4M proposal — fund coaches via district payroll, not invoiced ASB billings. (2) Stop billing ASB for Coach Clearance ($70 × ~80 coaches district-wide = $5,600/yr). (3) Roll Non-League Officials into the officials allocation increase (already in the $1.4M proposal). Net: makes the booster-relief case ~3× stronger than officials-only framing.
